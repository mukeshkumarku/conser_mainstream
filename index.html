VISION

Cultural contents including building, art forms, historic towns and cities and communities are most commonly seen as a commodity for tourism and business. Cultural resources are woven with people in their practices, belief systems, in their daily activities and that surviving association is important as it has its own value. Hence, as a conservator, I believe in the values of the resources and will be working on the development without disturbing the values of the heritage. Conservation Mainstream aims to work on strategies and management plans for the cultural resources in a more responsive way that will bring a change in the perspective of looking at the heritage. Opening up the wide range of heritage is one of our major focuses where the field requires a redefinition in the current world. From all the lessons learnt from my mentors, most importantly from Prof. Nalini Thakur and Prof. Balaji has given me a direction in approaching the cultural resources with thorough professionalism and ethics. Spreading awareness on the heritage is one of the main objectives of our practice which is achieved through a ‘dialogue’, by exchanging knowledge rather than providing direct facts to the people. Movement happen through dialogues than dictation is a belief in my practice. My Knowledge in architecture and Conservation compliments each other in expressing the values of heritage resources through my designs, research and decision making. The heritage resources will be approached scientifically with ground investigation and understood in depth for a better solution. Our team works with the vision of bringing the heritage in the mainstream development.
